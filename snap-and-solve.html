How It Works

What Happens Inside MathMate

From photo to guided explanation in a few seconds — here's every step the app takes on your behalf.

01

Capture the Problem

Students can take a photo of a problem, upload a question, or capture something from a notebook or worksheet — whatever format works for them.

02

MathMate Reads the Question

The app reads the problem directly from the image — no typing, no describing, no searching for the right mathematical language.

03

Identifies What Kind of Math Is Involved

MathMate recognises the topic — algebra, geometry, fractions, calculus, word problems — and prepares the explanation accordingly.

04

Prepares for Explanation

The problem is structured and verified so the student gets a clear, reliable path through — not just an answer, but the method behind it.

05

Student Chooses What Comes Next

Move into a step-by-step solution, save the problem for later revision, or jump straight into Quiz Mode to practice the same concept.

Education Research

Universal Design for Learning (UDL) supports the idea that learners need flexible ways to access and work with information. CAST's UDL Guidelines include supporting mathematical notation, using multiple media, offering action-oriented feedback, and helping learners organise information and monitor their own progress.

That is the learning logic behind Snap & Solve: students should not need to type perfectly or describe the problem perfectly before they can get help. MathMate removes that barrier so every student — regardless of how they express themselves — can begin from the actual problem in front of them.

Reference: CAST (2018). Universal Design for Learning Guidelines version 2.2. Wakefield, MA: CAST.
