iMathMate Tutorials
Feature by Feature

Everything you need to know to get the most out of iMathMate — from snapping your first problem to reading your personalised learning roadmap.

01
Getting Started

How to Snap & Solve a Problem

The fastest way to get help with any math problem. Point, snap, and get a full step-by-step explanation in seconds — no typing required.

1

Open the Scan tab

Tap the camera icon at the bottom of the screen to open the Snap & Solve tab.

2

Frame your problem

Hold your camera over any written or printed math problem. Make sure the text is well-lit and clearly visible inside the frame.

3

Tap to snap

Press the shutter button. iMathMate reads the problem and begins solving — usually within 5–10 seconds.

4

Read through the solution

Each step is explained clearly. Tap any step to expand it and read the full reasoning behind it.

Works with handwriting too

iMathMate reads neatly written handwritten problems as well as printed textbook questions.

Or upload from your gallery

Already have a photo? Tap the gallery icon to pick an existing image of a problem instead.

Tip: Good lighting makes a big difference. Avoid shadows falling across the problem, and keep the camera steady before tapping.

02
AI Tutor Chat

How to Use the AI Tutor Chat

Ask iMathMate anything — in plain English, French, or Arabic. The chat explains concepts, answers follow-up questions, and guides you through problems step by step.

1

Open the Chat tab

Tap the chat icon in the navigation bar. You'll enter a fresh conversation with iMathMate's AI tutor.

2

Type or paste your question

Ask anything: "Can you explain what a derivative is?", "Why do we flip the fraction when dividing?", or paste a problem directly.

3

Ask follow-up questions

iMathMate remembers the conversation. If you're still confused, ask "Can you explain that differently?" or "Show me another example."

4

Switch languages anytime

Go to Settings and choose English, French, or Arabic. The tutor will respond entirely in your chosen language.

Great questions to try

"Why does a negative times a negative equal a positive?" — iMathMate will explain with a clear analogy and worked example.

Available in 3 languages

English, French (Français), and Arabic (العربية) — with full right-to-left layout for Arabic learners.

Tip: The more context you give, the better the answer. Instead of "solve this," try "I don't understand why we do step 3 — can you explain it a different way?"

03
Quiz Mode

How to Take a Practice Quiz

Quizzes in iMathMate are not just tests — they're diagnostic tools that tell you what you know, what you don't, and what to focus on next.

1

Go to the Quiz tab

Tap "Quiz" in the bottom navigation. You'll see topic categories and difficulty levels.

2

Choose a topic or take the diagnostic

Pick any topic to practise, or take the full diagnostic quiz to get an overall assessment of your level.

3

Answer each question

Multiple-choice questions appear one at a time. Take your time — there's no timer.

4

Review wrong answers immediately

After each question, see the explanation — whether you got it right or wrong. Wrong answers trigger a mini-lesson on the spot.

Every wrong answer teaches

Getting something wrong in iMathMate isn't a failure — it's the system finding your exact learning gap. That's valuable data.

Results update your roadmap

Quiz scores feed directly into your personalised learning path — improving the accuracy of your recommendations each time.

Tip: Retake quizzes on the same topic every week. Watching your score improve over time is one of the most motivating things iMathMate shows you.

04
Progress & Roadmap

How to Read Your Learning Roadmap

Your roadmap updates automatically as you use the app. It tells you exactly where to focus next — based on everything iMathMate has learned about your skills.

1

Open the Progress tab

Tap the chart icon in the bottom bar to see your full progress dashboard.

2

Read your topic mastery scores

Each topic shows a percentage score. Green means strong, orange means review needed, red means learn this first.

3

Follow the "Next Steps" suggestions

Your roadmap highlights the 2–3 topics that will have the biggest impact on your improvement right now.

4

Track your streak

Daily usage builds your streak. A streak signals consistent practice — the single most reliable predictor of improvement.

Growth you can see

Topic-level charts show your score history over time. You'll see the impact of each study session reflected in real data.

Roadmap gets smarter over time

The more you use iMathMate, the more accurate your roadmap becomes. Early recommendations are good — later ones are excellent.

Tip: Share your Progress screen with a parent or teacher each week. It's a clear, honest picture of where you are and where you're heading.

05
Saved Gallery

How to Save Solutions for Later

Build your own personal revision library by saving solutions you want to return to. Perfect for exam preparation.

1

Solve any problem

Use Snap & Solve or Chat to get a solution to a problem you want to keep.

2

Tap the save icon

The bookmark icon appears at the top of any solution screen. Tap it to save the full solution to your Gallery.

3

Open the Saved tab

Find all your bookmarked solutions in the Saved tab — organised by date, with the original problem image and full explanation.

4

Review before exams

Scroll through your saved solutions the night before a test. Each one is a worked example of a concept you actually encountered.

Your personal revision bank

Over time, your Saved gallery becomes a curated set of worked examples — built from the actual problems you've faced.

Tip: Save problems you got wrong, not just ones you found hard. Coming back to past mistakes is one of the most effective revision strategies.
