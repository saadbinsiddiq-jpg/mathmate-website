Why MathMate

Why People Choose
to Work Here

We're a small team with a big mission — which means every person genuinely shapes the product.

🎓

Mission-Driven Work

Every line of code, every design decision, every word in an explanation reaches a student in a real moment of need. The impact is not abstract — it's in the reviews and the messages we get from users every week.

🚀

Small Team, Big Ownership

We're lean by design. That means less bureaucracy, faster decisions, and real ownership of the things you build. If you've ever wanted to see your ideas go from conversation to shipped feature in a week — this is that environment.

🌍

Fully Remote

MathMate is a remote-first team. Work from anywhere — we care about what you ship, not where you sit. Meetings are purposeful and async communication is the default.

📚

Learning is in Our DNA

We build a learning product, so it would be strange if we didn't value our own learning. Every team member gets a personal development budget, book allowance, and access to courses — no approval process required.

⚖️

Thoughtful Equity

Everyone who joins MathMate gets equity. We believe the people who build the product should share in what it becomes. We'll walk you through the details transparently during the offer process.

🛠️

No Red Tape

We hire people we trust and then trust the people we hire. No pointless status meetings, no micromanagement, no politics. If you want to change how something works, raise it — and watch it happen.

Our Hiring Process

What to Expect

Respectful of your time. No surprise rounds. No trick questions. We move fast.

01

Application

Send your CV and a short note on why MathMate. No cover letter templates, please — genuine is better.

02

30-min Intro Call

A conversation with a team member — not a screener. We want to understand you, not test you.

03

Practical Task

A short, paid task relevant to the role. We pay for your time. The task reflects real work — no abstract puzzles.

04

Offer

If it's a fit for both sides, we move to offer. Full transparency on equity, comp, and what joining actually looks like.
