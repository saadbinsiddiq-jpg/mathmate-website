What Students Can Save

Your In-App Learning Gallery

This is not a phone photo gallery. It's a dedicated learning space where every saved problem means something.

✅

A Solved Problem

Keep the solution alongside the method so you remember how you got there, not just what the answer was.

🧩

A Difficult Question

If a problem gave you trouble, save it immediately. Revisiting it a day or week later often unlocks new understanding.

📚

Homework & Worksheets

Questions from class, assignments, or practice sheets — everything you've worked through is worth keeping in one place.

❌

A Mistake You Want to Fix

Mistakes are the most valuable things to save. Reviewing a corrected error helps the right method stick much longer.

🔁

Topics You Want to Revisit

Problems connected to weak areas in your learning — save them now, practice them later when you're ready.

🗺️

Your Learning Journey

Why It Helps

Don't Let Useful Learning
Moments Disappear

Most students don't need to revise everything equally — they need to return to the questions that exposed a gap.

📌

A Useful Marker

A saved problem says "this is where I struggled" or "this is the type I should practice again." That context is more valuable than any mark.

🔄

The Real Learning Happens on Return

Math doesn't always become clear in one attempt. The Gallery makes the second visit easy — no searching, no re-solving from scratch.

📊

Supports Progress Tracking

Saved problems feed directly into Progress Tracking — so gaps identified today can become practice targets tomorrow.

🎯

Focused Exam Revision

Before a test, open the Gallery and work through your saved problems. It's a personalised revision list built from your actual experience.

🤔

Encourages Reflection

Revisiting saved work gives students a chance to ask: did I understand this? Can I do it again without help? That reflection is where growth lives.

🏗️

Builds a Personal Archive

The Gallery grows with the student — from their first saved problem to an organised record of everything they've worked through and overcome.

Education Research

CAST's Universal Design for Learning Guidelines include organising information and resources and enhancing learners' capacity to monitor progress. Save to Gallery supports both ideas — by helping students keep important problems in one place and return to them when they need revision.

The Education Endowment Foundation also highlights the importance of giving learners opportunities to reflect on strengths, areas for improvement, and ways to overcome current difficulties. A saved problem can support exactly that kind of reflection — it marks the moment a gap was exposed and gives the student a path back to address it.

References: CAST (2018). UDL Guidelines v2.2; Education Endowment Foundation — Metacognition and Self-Regulated Learning (2018).
